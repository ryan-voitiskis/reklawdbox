### Global section

[Screenshot: Global section toolbar showing numbered callouts from 1 to 14 across the top bar of the PERFORMANCE mode screen]

1. Select the PERFORMANCE mode.

2. Change the layout of the deck and waveform (2 deck/4 deck, horizontal/vertical or browse screen) (page 172).

3. Display the effect panel [FX] (page 136), GROOVE CIRCUIT panel [GC]\*1 (page 138), MIX POINT LINK panel [MP] (page 217), sampler deck [grid icon] (page 139), mixer panel [mixer icon] (page 141), recording panel [rec icon] (page 142), video panel [vid icon]\*2, and LIGHTING panel [light icon]\*2.

   \*1 To display the GROOVE CIRCUIT panel, open the [Preferences] window > [Extensions] category > [STEMS] tab, and turn on the following two functions.
   - [Enable the STEMS Function]
   - [Enable the GROOVE CIRCUIT Function]

   \*2 Click each tab on the [Preferences] window, and click the [Enable XXX Function] checkbox in the upper-left of the screen.

4. Turn on to enable Ableton Link. (page 148)

5. Open the PAD EDITOR window.
   Connect to DJ equipment supporting Pad Editor.
   For details on operating Pad Editor, refer to "PAD EDITOR Operation Guide" on the rekordbox website.

6. Open the setting window to use MIDI Learn. ([MIDI setting] window)
   Connect to equipment which has the MIDI control function.
   For details on operating MIDI Learn, refer to "MIDI LEARN Operation Guide" on the rekordbox website.

7. Display the [INFO] window.

8. Activate the computer and display your subscription plan applied to rekordbox.

9. Display the [Preferences] window.

10. Adjust the volume level from MASTER OUT.
    When connecting to DJ equipment compatible with this feature, [PC icon] is shown for setting PC MASTER OUT on or off.
    - [ON]: MASTER OUT sound outputs from both the connected DJ equipment and computer's internal speaker.
    - [OFF]: MASTER OUT sound outputs from the connected DJ equipment only.

    For compatible DJ equipment, refer to FAQ on the rekordbox website.

11. The volume level from MASTER OUT is indicated.

12. The audio dropout meter (audio dropouts that have reached 100%) is displayed.

13. When the computer is unplugged from the mains power supply, the remaining battery life of the computer is displayed.

14. The current time is displayed.

### Track information panel

[Screenshot: Track information panel showing artwork, track name, artist, BPM, key, time display, key sync controls, beat sync, full waveform with phrase display, and current key information]

1. The track's artwork image is displayed.
   Point on the artwork image with the mouse cursor to display [eject icon], and then click [eject icon] to unload the track.

2. The track name, artist name, original BPM and key are displayed.

3. Remaining time ( [-] ) and elapsed time are displayed.

4. Turn down the key by a semitone. (Semitone Down)

5. Turn on/off the Key Sync function.

6. Turn up the key by a semitone. (Semitone Up)

7. Turn on/off the Beat Sync function (page 162).

8. Set the BPM to the original, double, or half of the sync master BPM. (SYNC RATE)

9. Display the current cue position.

10. The full waveform, cue point, memory cue point, hot cue point, phrase, and Lighting scenes are displayed.
    Click to shift the playback position to the position you clicked.
    The waveform color can be changed on the [Preferences] window. (page 89)

11. The key sync function or the current key of the track is displayed.
    Double-click to reset the key if you have changed it using semitone up/down function.
    To change the key display format, click the [Preferences] window > [View] category > [Display Type] tab > [Key display format].
    When the master tempo is off, the key display will also change if the tempo (BPM) is changed using the Beat Sync function, etc.

12. The difference from the original key is displayed.

13. Set the deck as a Master Player for the Beat Sync function.

### JOG panel

Click [grid icon] to display the performance pad and the JOG panel if the [GRID EDIT] panel is displayed.

[Screenshot: JOG panel showing a circular jog wheel display with numbered callouts for deck number, navigation arrows, INT/DVS selector, CUE button, BPM display, speed adjustment, SLIP button, quantize button, AU/MA buttons, play button, pitch bend, tempo range, and master tempo]

1. Click [AU] or [MA] to switch the buttons.

   [Screenshot: Close-up of AU/MA button area showing deck number 4, navigation arrows, and AU/MA buttons]

   : Set the loop length by the specified number of beats. (Auto Beat Loop)

   [Screenshot: Close-up of MA mode showing IN, OUT, RELOOP buttons, and AU/MA buttons]

   : Set real-time cues and loop in/out points, and cancel/resume a loop playback. (Manual Loop) (page 157)

2. When playback is paused, a cue point is set to the current position. Click and hold to continue playback. (page 155)

3. Select the DVS mode.

4. You can set information associated with the platter on the [Preferences] window > [View] category > [Display Type] tab > [Switch JOG Display].
   - [Current CUE / SLIP]: A red marker indicates the position of the Cue point. When [SLIP] is on, the inner ring turns red, and a yellow marker indicates the position of the Cue point.
   - [HOT CUE COUNTDOWN]: The distance to Hot Cue within 5 laps is indicated. It changes the platter displaying according to the distance to the Hot Cue.

5. Display the current BPM.

6. Adjust the playback speed. The playback speed can be changed by clicking [+]/[-]. (page 150)

7. Perform without breaking the rhythm of the track. (slip mode) (page 161)

8. Set a loop in point, loop out point, Hot Cue, and Cue automatically according to the beat position. (quantize function) (page 154)

9. Start or pause playback. (page 150)

10. Display the playback speed changed rate (%).

11. Keep clicking it for pitch bend during the playback.

12. The adjustable range for playing speed is displayed. (TEMPO RANGE)

13. Adjust the playback speed without changing the pitch. (Master Tempo) (page 150)
    When you shift the key on a track information panel, the display here is changed to [KEY RESET]. Click to reset the key.

### Performance pads

If the [GRID EDIT] panel is displayed, click [grid icon] to switch to the performance pad.
By switching the pad, you can use various functions.
In the PAD mode, click the function name displayed in the lower side of the pad. This allows you to select various functions to be assigned to the performance pad.
[HOT CUE] (page 127) / [PAD FX] (page 128) / [SLICER] (page 129) / [BEAT JUMP] (page 130) / [BEAT LOOP] (page 130) / [KEYBOARD] (page 131) / [KEY SHIFT] (page 131) / [SEQ. CALL] (page 132) / [ACT. CENSR] (page 133) / [MEMORY CUE] (page 134)

#### [HOT CUE] mode

After saving Hot Cues, you can call the specific playback position quickly and mark on the full waveform or enlarged waveform.
For details on how to operate, see "Using a Hot Cue" (page 159).

[Screenshot: HOT CUE pad display showing 8 pads labeled A through H with timestamps, loop indicators, and scroll/delete controls]

1. Hot Cues are displayed.
   If you click an empty pad, the current playback position is saved as a Hot Cue.
   If you click a pad with a Hot Cue, the playback position jumps to the Hot Cue position.
   Right-click the Hot Cue pad to enter comments and change the color.

2. Shows when a loop point is saved. (HOT CUE LOOP)
   Click it to set to an active loop. Its color then changes to red.

3. Scroll the display.

4. Point at the pad with the mouse cursor, and then click [X] to delete the Hot Cue from the pad.

#### [PAD FX] mode

For details on how to operate, see "Using PAD FX" (page 182).

[Screenshot: PAD FX display showing 8 effect pads with names like SWEEP, SLIP LOOP, BACKSPIN, ECHO, DELAY, FILTER LFO with beat values, scroll control, settings gear icon, and bank selector showing banks 1 and 2]

1. While holding the click, the effect is applied.
   For the Release FX, turn on or off by clicking.

2. Change the FXs and FX parameters assigned to each performance pad.
   - There are 4 parameters; [BEAT FX], [SOUND COLOR FX], [SCENE FX], and [RELEASE FX].
     In a parameter [BEAT FX], you can change the beat of the FX.
   - In the Release FX pad, set [HOLD ON/OFF] to [ON] to turn on by holding, or set [HOLD ON/OFF] to [OFF] to turn on/off by clicking.

3. Scroll the display.

4. Switch the bank.
   You can save effect settings on two banks.

#### [SLICER] mode

For details on how to operate, see "Using a slicer" (page 197).

[Screenshot: SLICER pad display showing 8 numbered slices (1-8), loop button, navigation arrows, slice length selector showing 8, and roll length selector showing 1/2]

1. While clicking, roll playback of a sliced and assigned section occurs. (Playback of the section is repeated only while clicking the pad.)

2. The same sliced section is played.

3. The range of the slicer is shifted and moved.

4. Change the length of the slice.

5. Change the length of the roll.

#### [BEAT JUMP] mode

For details on how to operate, see "Using the beat jump function" (page 163).

[Screenshot: BEAT JUMP pad display showing directional arrows with values FINE, 1/8, 1/4, 1/2 on the left and 1, 2, 4, 8 on the right, with scroll control]

1. The playback position jumps to the displayed length (beat or bar) and direction.
   During the loop play, the loop position moves.

2. Scroll the display.

#### [BEAT LOOP] mode

[Screenshot: BEAT LOOP pad display showing 8 pads with beat lengths 1/64, 1/32, 1/16, 1/8, 1/4, 1/2, 1, 2 and scroll control]

1. Specify a beat length for a loop play from the current playback position.
   Click again to cancel the specifying.

2. Scroll the display.

#### [KEYBOARD] mode

For details on how to operate, see "To use [KEYBOARD]" (page 165).

[Screenshot: KEYBOARD pad display showing 8 pads with semitone values +12, +11, +10, +9, +8, +7, +6, +5, scroll control, settings gear icon, HOT CUE selector, and HOT CUE chooser]

1. Shift the key by a semitone when using a HOT CUE.

2. Scroll the display.

3. Switch to the HOT CUE screen to select a HOT CUE.

4. Select a HOT CUE.

#### [KEY SHIFT] mode

For details on how to operate, see "To use [KEY SHIFT]" (page 165).

[Screenshot: KEY SHIFT pad display showing 8 pads with semitone values +12, +11, +10, +9, +8, +7, +6, +5, and scroll control]

1. Shift the key by a semitone.

2. Scroll the display.

#### [SEQ. CALL] (SEQUENCE CALL) mode

For details on how to operate, see "Loading a sequence to a deck (SEQUENCE LOAD) " (page 196).

[Screenshot: SEQ. CALL pad display showing 8 pattern slots P1 through P8 with bar lengths (2Bars, 1Bars)]

1. Play the saved sequence.

#### [ACT. CENSR] (ACTIVE CENSOR) mode

You can apply an effect to specified sections of a track. When the playback position reaches a specified section, the effect is automatically applied.

- You can specify multiple sections of a track.
- During reverse playback, the effect is not applied.

For details on how to operate, see "Using ACTIVE CENSOR" (page 203).

[Screenshot: ACTIVE CENSOR pad display showing effect selection (REV ROLL, TRANS, ECHO, V.BRAKE), IN/OUT point controls, CLEAR button, ON/OFF toggle, navigation arrows, and beat parameter controls]

1. Select an effect.

2. Set the beginning point for the effect to be applied. [IN icon] is shown on the enlarged waveform.
   When the playback position is in the specified range, [IN ADJ] is displayed and you can change the beginning point.

3. Set the ending point at which the effect will no longer be applied.
   When the playback position is in the specified range, [OUT ADJ] is displayed and you can change the ending point.

4. Clear the specified section. You can click when the playback position reaches to the specified section.

5. Jump to the beginning of the specified section (IN point).

6. When selecting [TRANS] or [V.BRAKE], change the beat parameter.

7. Turn on/off the active censor mode.

#### [MEMORY CUE] mode

For details on how to operate, see "To save a cue point" (page 156) or "To save the loop" (page 158).

[Screenshot: MEMORY CUE pad display showing 10 memory cue/loop points with timestamps and colored markers, loop indicators, MEMORY save button, and delete X button]

1. Change the color of the Cue marker.

2. Display the saved cue or loop.
   Left-click the time to jump to the point. Playback pauses.
   Right-click the time to enter a comment.

3. Shows when a loop point is saved. (MEMORY LOOP)
   Click it to set to an active loop. Its color then changes to red.

4. Save a cue or loop.

5. Point at the pad with the mouse cursor, and then click [X] to delete the saved Cue or loop.

### [GRID/PHRASE EDIT] panel

After selecting a deck, click [grid icon] to switch to the [GRID/PHRASE EDIT] panel.

[Screenshot: GRID/PHRASE EDIT panel showing GRID EDIT section on the left with beat position, BPM display, TAP button, grid adjustment controls, and PHRASE EDIT section on the right with CUT, phrase type, and CLEAR controls]

1. Set the nearest grid position to [1.1Bars].

2. Shift the playback position (a red vertical line at the center of the waveform) to the first beat of bar. (page 154)

3. Move the beat grid to the left by 1 msec / 3 msec.

4. Move the beat grid to the right by 1 msec / 3 msec.

5. Undo your last action in the [GRID EDIT] panel.

6. Redo the action which you undid in the [GRID EDIT] panel.

7. Display and change the Auto Gain value detected by track analysis. (Auto Gain)
   When the [Preferences] window > [Controller] category > [Mixer] tab > [Auto Gain] is set to [Enable], this value is displayed.

8. Start or pause playback.

9. Display the distance of beat grid at the playback position (a red vertical line at the center of the waveform) as BPM. You can also enter BPM manually to change the distance of beat grid.

10. Change the beat grid according to BPM determined by the click interval of [TAP].

11. Narrow the beat grid distance by 1 msec / 3 msec when [fine adjustment icon] is turned on.

12. Double/halve the BPM. The length of each beat grid distance is halved/doubled.

13. Select the whole track as an adjustment range of the beat grid.
    When switching to the [GRID EDIT] panel, the whole track is already selected and this button is not available. The adjustment range is changed to the whole track when [fine adjustment icon] is turned on and the range is set to the current position or later.

14. Re-set the beat grid from the current position (a red vertical line at the center of the waveform) according to the BPM determined.

15. Widen the beat grid distance by 1 msec / 3 msec when [fine adjustment icon] is turned on.

16. Turn the metronome sound on/off and change its volume to one of three levels.
    Metronome sound starts with the playback track, synchronized with the beat grid.

17. Enable/Disable Track Analysis, Key Analysis, and Beatgrid Adjustment. (page 153)
    If you set it to disable, [P] is displayed on the track list.

18. Edit each phrase which is displayed in the track information panel by loading the track analyzed with [Phrase].
    - After you click [CUT] to light up, click on the phrase to be divided.
    - To change the phrase type, click [phrase type icon].
    - To delete all phrases, click [CLEAR].
    - To adjust the position of the phrase, click the phrase and drag the divided position (white line).
    - To display the phrase, open the [Preferences] window > [View] category > [Layout] tab, and select [Phrase (Full Waveform)] (page 223).
    - For details about [PHRASE EDIT] panel, refer to "PHRASE EDIT operation guide" on the rekordbox website.

### Effect panel

Click [FX] in the global section (page 123). The following screens are about [BEAT FX]. For details on how to operate, see "Using effect features" (page 176).

- Single mode: Apply one effect.

[Screenshot: Single mode effect panel showing deck selector (1-4, S, M), mode switch, effect selector (ECHO), parameter controls (FEEDBACK, FILTER, FREEZE), FX LEVEL/DEPTH knob, beat selector, Release FX (VINYL BRAKE), BPM display, and AUTO/TAP buttons]

- Multiple mode: Apply multiple effects at the same time.

[Screenshot: Multiple mode effect panel showing deck selector, mode switch, three effect slots (REVERB, ECHO, PITCH) with individual parameter controls, Release FX (VINYL BRAKE), beat selector, BPM display, and AUTO/TAP buttons]

1. Select a deck to be applied with an effect.
   - [1] through [4]: Apply the effect to decks 1 through 4.
   - [S]: Apply the effect to the sampler.
   - [M]: Apply the effect to MASTER OUT.

2. Switch between the single mode and multiple mode.

3. Turn on/off the effect.

4. Select an effect.

5. Save multiple parameter settings that are adjusted in the single mode.
   You can call the saved parameter settings when you select the effect.
   The position of FX LEVEL/DEPTH is not saved.

6. Turn on/off the Release FX.

7. Select a Release FX.

8. Set BPM.

9. Adjust the volume balance between the original sound and the sound with effects. (FX LEVEL/DEPTH)

10. Change the number of beats of the effect.

11. Adjust the parameter for each effect.

12. Turn on/off the parameter for the effect.

13. Change the number of beats of the Release FX.

### GROOVE CIRCUIT panel

[Screenshot: GROOVE CIRCUIT panel showing DRUM LOOP BANK A/B selector, 4 DRUM SWAP SLOTs with waveforms, SINGLE/MULTI mode selector, GAIN control, drum preset load/save buttons, DRUM RELEASE FX (VINYL BRAKE) with beat selector, and a DRUM CAPTURE waveform display]

1. DRUM LOOP BANK
   Switch between BANK A/B for 4 DRUM SWAP SLOTs.

2. DRUM SWAP SLOT
   Play/stop the drum loop audio source. The dots displayed below the waveform indicate the positions of the grid.

3. Playback mode of DRUM SWAP
   - DRUM SWAP SINGLE MODE (SINGLE)
     Select this option when replacing the drums of a track loaded on the deck with the drum loop audio source of the DRUM SWAP SLOT.
   - DRUM SWAP MULTI MODE (MULTI)
     Select this option when adding drum loop audio sources for individual instruments such as bass drum, snare, and hihat to a track loaded on the deck.

4. DRUM SWAP GAIN
   Adjust the volume of 8 DRUM SWAP SLOTs in DRUM LOOP BANK A/B.

5. Loading Drum Preset
   You can replace all DRUM SWAP SLOTs in DRUM LOOP BANK A/B with different drum loop audio sources at once.

6. Saving Drum Preset
   You can save the loaded drum loop audio sources of DRUM LOOP BANK A/B at once.

7. DRUM RELEASE FX
   Apply the RELEASE FX to the DRUMS Stem of a track loaded on the deck or to the drum loop audio source of the DRUM SWAP SLOT playing in DRUM SWAP SINGLE MODE.

8. DRUM CAPTURE
   Extract the DRUMS Stem of a track loaded on the deck and use it for DRUM SWAP SLOT.

### Sampler deck

Click [grid icon] in the global section (page 123).
For details on operation, see "Using the sampler deck" (page 188).

[Screenshot: Sampler deck showing 8 sampler slots with waveforms and names (Raggahorn, 01 Aaah #1, Laser #1, Laser #2, 26 Come On Yeah, 07 Here We Go, Airhorn, Gunshot), GAIN knob, CUE button, output level display, BANK selector, OUTPUT channel selector, quantize button, BPM SYNC, MASTER button, sampler/OSC switch, and BPM adjustment with +/- buttons]

1. Play the sampler.
   To stop playback, click this with pressing the [Shift] key on the computer keyboard.

2. Output the sampler deck to your headphones.

3. Adjust the volume of the whole sampler deck.

4. Adjust the output level of the whole sampler deck.

5. Sets of samplers are saved in four banks. (Sampler bank)

6. Select an output channel for the sampler deck during the external mixer mode.

7. Set the quantize to on / off. (Sampler quantize)

8. Set SYNC to the sampler deck.

9. Set the sampler deck as the master in the sync mode.

10. Switch between the sampler and OSC sampler.

11. Adjust BPM for playback of the sampler.
    Click [-]/[+] to change the BPM.

#### Sequencer panel

When displaying the sampler deck, the sequencer panel is also displayed.
For details on operation, see "Using a sequencer" (page 193).

[Screenshot: Sequencer panel showing record button, play button, SAVE button, pattern selector (PATTERN 1), bar length selector (2Bars), beat counter with position markers, MUTE button, ERASE button, and metronome controls]

1. Record the sampler sequence.

2. Play the sampler sequence.

3. Save the recorded sampler sequence.

4. Indicate how the sequence is saved. (Sequence slot)
   A sequence name in white indicates that the sequence is saved. A grayed out sequence name indicates that the sequence is not saved. A grayed out sequence name with a red circle indicates that the sequence is temporarily recorded.
   Up to eight sequences can be saved.

5. Change the sequence length.

6. Indicates sequence length and beats. (Beat Counter)
   During recording or playback of a sequence, a marker moves to indicate the playback position.

7. Mute the sampler sequence per sampler slot. ([Sequence MUTE mode])

8. Delete the sampler sequence per sampler slot. ([Sequence ERASE mode])

9. Turn the metronome sound on/off.
   Metronome sound starts with the playback track, synchronized with the beat grid.

10. Change the metronome volume to one of three levels.
